What Is the Niko-niko ?

Apr 21st, 2013

The Niko-niko calendar is an Agile practice which aims to detect patterns of change of mood of a team over time.

The technic is very easy: at the end of the day, each team member paste a sticker on a calendar to tell if he had a good day at work.

In other word, you probably ever went back to home from work and feel that you had a bad day, or you did not feel “productive”. That’s a red sticker.

After some time, you’ll see some color being dominant, it will give an insight of the mood of the team.

It is a good idea to take a look at the Niko-niko calendar during the Agile retrospective.

It is not exact science, it is just a simple tool to get some more informations about the team.

The Niko-niko calendar is also know as “happiness index”. Also note that “niko” means “smile” in the japanese language.

The first mention of the Niko-niko calendar is from an article from Akinori SAKATA in 2006.

The Idea

Mar 31st, 2013

It’s been a while since I wanted to build a project of my own. On a daily basis, I give advices to IT companies about technologies, methodologies and strategies to build awesome products. And now it is my turn !

So I finally took the time to build my first side project: TeamMood !

I will not explain what the project is about in this post, I prefer to let you discover the idea through the very first MVP which is a simple ”coming soon” page and let you give me a lot of feedback.

Basically, the idea scratched me since many months because I feel that it can bring a lot in some companies that I work with. But it took me a lot of time before starting to build something, probably because I thought that if I begin to do this, I’d have to pull it to the end, ie some kind of commitment.

And yes, I use LeanStartup to explore the idea, because I don’t know if it will be a good one and if people will be interested. So let’s learn with this first MVP and we’ll see :-)
